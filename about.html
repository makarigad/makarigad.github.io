About the Project

Makari Gad Hydropower Limited (MGHL) is the proponent of the Makari Gad Hydroelectric Project. As a limited company registered under the Company Act of Nepal, MGHL is dedicated to developing sustainable energy solutions, with this project being a key testament to that commitment.

The Makari Gad Hydroelectric Project (MGHEP) is a 10 MW run-of-river scheme located in Apihimal Rural Municipality, Darchula District of Sudurpashchim Province. It was developed by Makari Gad Hydropower Pvt. Ltd. under a BOOT (Build-Own-Operate-Transfer) model. Construction was completed and the plant was commissioned on March 11, 2023. MGHEP is now operational and connected to the national grid at Balanch Substation via a 33 kV transmission line.

The project's water source, the Makari Gad, is a small, perennial left-sided tributary of the Chameliya River. It originates from the Chaupode Dhar and Dharmaghar Dhar, meeting the Chameliya River at Makarigad Gaun village. The total catchment area of the Makari Gad at the proposed headworks site is 37.8 km2.

The project infrastructure consists of a diversion weir across the Makari river, an intake structure, an approach canal, a desanding basin that also functions as a head pond, a 3.2 km long surface penstock along the right bank of the Makari river, and a powerhouse with its tailrace outfall into the Chameliya river, upstream of their confluence.
