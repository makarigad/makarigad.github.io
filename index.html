Makari Gad Hydroelectric Project (10 MW)

Apihimal-5, Darchula, Sudurpaschim Province, Nepal

About the Project

Makari Gad Hydropower Private Limited, hereinafter referred to as MGHPL, is the proponent of the Makari Gad Hydroelectric Project (MGHEP). MGHPL is a private limited company registered under the Company Act of Nepal.

The Makari Gad Hydroelectric Project (MGHEP) is a ‘Run-of-the-River’ (RoR) type project located in Apihimal Rural Municipality of Darchula district, Sudurpashchim Province, Nepal. It has an installed capacity of 10 MW and is contracted to supply 69.756 GWh of energy to the Nepal Electricity Authority (NEA).

The project survey area lies between latitude 29°48’03”N and 29°46’54”N, and longitude 80°52’47”E and 80°50’33”E. The Makari Gad is a left-bank tributary of the Chameliya River. It is a perennial stream originating from Chaupode Dhar and Dharmaghar Dhar, joining Chameliya at Makarigad Gaun village. The catchment area at the proposed headworks site is 37.8 sq. km.

The project includes a diversion weir across Makari River, an intake structure, an approach canal, a desanding basin cum head pond, a 3.2 km surface penstock along the right bank, and a powerhouse that discharges into the Chameliya River upstream of the confluence. The design discharge is 1.35 m³/s with a net head of 862.35 m.

Generated power is transmitted to the 33/132 kV substation at Balanch via a 27.2 km long, 33 kV single circuit transmission line.

The project is road-accessible up to the powerhouse. The access route includes 254 km of black-topped road (Dhangadi–Gokuleshwor section of Mahakali Highway), 25 km of black-topped Gokuleshwor–Bitule road, and 25 km of seasonal earthen road from Bitule to Makarigad. A 2 km ropeway is used to transport materials from the powerhouse to the dam site.
